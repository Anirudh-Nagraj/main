The COEIT research website was identified by people to be a little gaudy on the eye and have some prevailing issues that make the user shy away from the current website. Hence the attempt to redesign.

Shendelzare and her bid to improve usability

Shendelzare, is the lead when it comes to the University of Maryland, Baltimore County's College of Engineering and Information technology's (Abbv COEIT) research website

Her issues stem from the decreased usability of the COEIT research website. The team of another student and myself decided to take matters into our own hands as a term research project and make the website usable, user friendly via user researh and incorporating UX methodologies.

Requirements

Shendelzare wants increased usability and improved traffic on the website.

Initial usability concerns

Shendelzare outlined some usability concerns and gave us a document highlighting the issues. Concerns were related to inconsistent content , limited to no user engagement , content being verbose , no clear pathways for research content , and the site not being designed for an assorment of users.

So what did we do?

We had our work cut out. As researchers in this project, we had to understand the W's. (Who are the users?, What problem are we solving?, How are we solving the problem?)
