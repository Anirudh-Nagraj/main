USPS.com® Website Usability Study at UserWorks, Inc.

What's this about?

While at UserWorks, I conducted a study to generate recommendations for the United States Postal Service website. The personnel at USPS wanted to check the interaction of users on new pages that included international shipping. They also wanted us to analyze features that could be added in the future, per what the users mentioned.

I like the images. They are very easy on the eye and convey a lot of information.

Study Structure

24 usability test sessions conducted remotely in November 2021.​

Each session was approximately 80 minutes.

Sessions were split into those conducted on mobile and those on desktop, along with those mailing and shipping primarily for personal (consumer) reasons, business reasons, or a bit of both (hybrid).

USER INTERVIEWS

4 participants in each category were recruited, for a total of 4 mobile personal (consumer) sessions, 4 mobile business, 4 mobile hybrid (with 1 participating using a tablet), 4 desktop personal, 4 desktop business, and 4 desktop hybrid. Other participant categories, such as business size (where applicable), age, education, location (urban, suburban, or rural), state, and how often they visited USPS.com® were recruited to the best possible mix.

Some Findings from the Interviews

From the exhaustive interviews conducted, interesting findings emerged.

  • The participants in this study sent international mail and packages very infrequently.
    • This was intentional; we wanted to obtain feedback from those who would most benefit from these additions – those to whom this information is new.
    • Most business participants were sending documents or printed materials, rather than manufactured parts or goods.
    • Going to the Post Office was generally not seen as a hassle because the Post Office was either close by or worth the travel, given how infrequently they sent internationally.
    • Usually, participants wanted to avoid going to the Post Office to avoid lines or “unfriendly” staff, rather than due to travel distance.

  • Most saw USPS.com® as a cohesive website, rather than a “portal” of sorts.

Recommendations

Impact

Recommendations to improve the overall User Experience of users visiting USPS.com®.

Simpler navigation across pages.

Placement of images where required for improved readability

Next Steps

Depending on the application and development of the features and improvements provided by the participants, long-term observation would be beneficial to measure the impact and success of the proposed changes. Without numbers, it becomes difficult for UX Researchers to measure the true effect of the recommendations provided

Takeaways

80-minute interviews are exhaustive, and recruiting is a lengthy process. As a researcher, it is paramount to have enormous patience until the study is complete. Qualitative coding helps in analyzing the findings. One key takeaway is that each participant's different views can lead to different perspectives. Bias plays a significant role in the type of answer the user provides. No-shows in long-drawn studies can prove tricky, so having back-up participants proves helpful.
