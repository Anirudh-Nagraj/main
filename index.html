About

I am Anirudh Nagraj, an aspiring User Experience Professional. From a very young age, I have been drawn towards accessibility, and how I can be of help to people. Before I stepped foot into the world of UX, I was employed as a Software Developer at LTI. That gig was cool but it rocked me to my core, and significantly altered my thought process. I started questioning stuff instead of simply going ahead with specifics. This led to me to touch my passion waters again and pursue a master’s degree in Human-Centered Computing

UX Research & Accessibility Design

I have been involved in research on accessibility from the time I joined the university and I am currently investigating the navigational habits of people who are blind. I intend to look at ways in which a user-centered approach can increase accessibility. Through the data collected, I intend to look at ways in which participatory design can be incorporated with such an audience. My projects have taken me from blind schools to house-holds with acute water shortages to sitting on an office chair, working on company projects, to sitting in a classroom again ; only this time, I ponder on how to make people’s lives easier through better design through my efforts in the world of UX.
