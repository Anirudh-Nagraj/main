My Work

The projects below represent the work done in the space of accessibility, HCI and UX research, UX research for the web for various teams within the university, and my master's thesis which is also in the space of accessibility.

THE TRIDENT

A study to build a navigational aid for runners who are blind using Human Centered Design Methods and User Research.

Research Methods: Literature Reviews, Interviews, Affinity Map, Scenario Development, Competitive Analysis, Personas, BodyStorming.

UMBC - COEIT RESEARCH

Project to provide recommendations for the univeristy's College of Engineering and Information Technology's RESEARCH website through extensive user research.

Research Methods: Literature Reviews, Interviews, Contextual Inquiries, Competitive Analysis, Heuristic Evaluations, Rapid Prototyping.

SHARED SERVICES CENTER WEBSITE REDESIGN

The Shared Services Center Team found it difficult to attract users to their website. This is a project to redesing the existing website using user research and front end web development.

Research Methods: Literature Reviews, Interviews, NPS Score, Statistical Analysis, Heuristic Evaluations

B-AID - THE NAV APP FOR THE BLIND

Globally, millions of people suffer from vision related issues. Passionate as I am for accessibility, the project revolves around creating a mobile application prototype for individuals who are visually impaired relating to their navigation.

Research Methods: Literature Reviews, Interviews, Field Studies, Storyboards, Affinity Map, Empathy map, Personas, Rapid Prototyping.

Built with a lot of thought and passion by Anirudh.
