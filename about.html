Me in a nutshell...

Do you know? I've been fortunate enough to help people who need assistance my entire life. Motivation? People who need increased assistance. My aim has always been to make things as easy as possible for people with disabilities; a feeling directed towards the community. This drive only increased when I joined the University of Maryland, Baltimore County as a Human-Centered Computing graduate (Go Retrievers!! üê∂). As a HCI Research Assistant, working on accessibility projects has taught me loads of research methods, and more importantly, to put empathy at the forefront while not losing sight of my passion. Under the supervision of Dr. Ravi Kuber, we submitted two papers to The International ACM SIGACCESS Conference on Computers and Accessibility. Our paper titled "Investigating the navigational habits of people who are blind in India" is set to appear at ASSETS 2021. I worked on a collaborative project funded by TOYOTA to build a wearable belt and increase assistance for blind runners in their path. Two years of research have taught me that anything is possible; perseverance is the key.

Parallel to working as a HCI Research Assistant, I worked as a contracted UX Researcher with the Shared Services Center team of the University. I conducted Heuristic evaluations, conducted contextual analysis as a part of my research to revamp the website design that yielded successful results.

Before my master's journey began, I worked as a Software Engineer in the UI/UX team with L&T Infotech in Bangalore, India. I know what it means to wear the developer's hat while designing a product and the sweetness of interacting with peers while plying my trade in a cross-functional setup.

Me and UX

By leveraging UX Research, I get into the minds of the users whom businesses serve. I help identify the clients and stakeholders needs, thereby looking to increase the product's value at hand. I like to work with multiple methods (Interviews, Contextual Inquiries, Personas, etc.) to triangulate findings, backing up qualitative data with quantitative and vice-versa. The technique used in the research is dependent on the requirements of the businesses, stakeholders, and users. P.S. I am also a huge fan of Web Accessibility and I will take the IAAP- CPACC certification soon. With solid hardware and software research expirience under my belt, I am adept at human-human interaction, bringing the best of the information from the users.
